A Continuum Hartree–Fock Many-Body Solver

A from-scratch self-consistent mean-field solver for interacting 2D electrons, built around a linear-response → instability → self-consistency diagnostic pipeline.

  • Role — Solo developer & researcher
  • Stack — Python · NumPy / SciPy
  • Scale — ~10,500 lines · NERSC / SLURM

A self-consistent solution of the solver, shown three ways — charge-density crystal (left), spin direction (middle), and spin amplitude (right). The interacting electron liquid spontaneously orders into a triangular crystal with a nontrivial spin texture.

This is the mean-field engine behind my correlated-electron research. It solves the Hartree–Fock / self-consistent-field equations for a two-dimensional electron gas with Rashba spin–orbit coupling and Coulomb interaction, entirely from scratch.

The design is a tiered diagnostic pipeline: compute the bare susceptibility χ₀(q) to find where the electron liquid wants to order, run a Stoner instability analysis to rank candidate orders, then solve the full self-consistent field for the winners — with band-topology diagnostics (Berry-curvature Chern numbers by two independent methods) on top.

The emphasis throughout is numerical rigor: every quantity is checked against an analytic limit or a second method (for example, the bare susceptibility χ₀ is checked against the analytic 2D Lindhard result), and results are verified for basis- and grid-convergence before anything is trusted. Runs at scale on NERSC via SLURM.

What it demonstrates

  • Python · NumPy / SciPy
  • Self-consistent mean-field / Hartree–Fock
  • Linear-response theory
  • Berry curvature & Chern numbers
  • Numerical convergence discipline
  • SLURM / NERSC HPC
Status. A computational-methods showcase — the research physics results are unpublished and deliberately omitted. Code to be released later.
