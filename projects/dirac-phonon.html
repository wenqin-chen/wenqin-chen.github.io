Giant Phonon Magnetism in Dirac Materials

Two first-author theories explaining why lattice vibrations acquire anomalously large magnetic moments in doped Dirac semimetals.

  • Role — First author (×2)
  • Venue — Phys. Rev. B 111 (2025) + arXiv:2505.09732
  • Method — Analytical theory

The predicted phonon magnetic moment (in Bohr magnetons) grows steeply with doping away from the Dirac node and vanishes at the node — a Fermi-surface effect, shown across the experimental doping window.

Phonons — quantized lattice vibrations — normally carry no magnetic moment. But in Dirac and Weyl semimetals, the circular motion of the ions couples to the material's Berry curvature, and phonons can acquire surprisingly large magnetic moments.

In two complementary first-author papers I built the theory of this effect in doped Dirac semimetals: a gauge-theory mechanism (Physical Review B 111, 035126, 2025) and a geometric-origin picture that ties the moment directly to the band Berry curvature (arXiv:2505.09732, 2025). The theory predicts how the moment scales with doping — vanishing at the Dirac node and growing steeply into the experimental window (figure) — and its predictions now guide ultrafast-spectroscopy and transport experiments with collaborators at Los Alamos (CINT) and UC Irvine.
