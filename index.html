Computational Physicist

Wenqin Chen

Machine learning & many-body theory for quantum materials

I build computational and machine-learning models for hard problems in quantum matter — from transformer neural networks that solve the quantum many-body problem to the theory behind new magnetic and superconducting states. Physics PhD candidate at the University of Washington (advisors Di Xiao and Ting Cao), moving to industry in 2027.

  • First-author papers in Physical Review B; co-author in Physical Review Letters and Nature Materials; 180+ citations.
  • Research internships at Los Alamos National Laboratory (Theoretical Division) and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory.
  • 5+ years of Python scientific computing, large-scale numerical modeling, and GPU/HPC (JAX, NumPy/SciPy, SLURM).
  • Now bringing that toolkit — deep learning, large-scale computation, and numerical modeling — to industry.
